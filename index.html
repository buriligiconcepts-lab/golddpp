Risk management rules
Never risk more than 1-2% of your account on any single trade. If you hit 3 losses in a row — stop trading for the day.
MGC micro gold = $1 per point. GC full gold = $10 per point. A 10-point stop on GC = $100 risk per contract.
Always wait for a 2:1 minimum R:R before entering. Your edge only works if you let winners run and cut losers fast.
Only trade during London (3am-12pm ET) or NY (8:30am-1:30pm ET) sessions. Avoid low-volume periods.

What is Delta Hedging?
When market makers sell options they take on directional risk. To stay neutral they buy or sell the underlying futures to offset their delta exposure. This forced hedging activity creates predictable buying and selling pressure at specific price levels — and that is your edge.

Contracts of net short delta exposure
Dealers are net short delta — meaning they need to buy futures as price rises to stay hedged. This creates natural support on dips.

How to use delta hedging in your trades
Positive GEX regime: Price is attracted to max pain. Fades and mean reversion trades work best. Avoid chasing breakouts — dealers will push price back.
Negative GEX regime: Momentum and trend trades work best. Dealers amplify every move. Breakouts tend to follow through. Widen your stops.
GEX flip zone: Most volatile area. Price at max pain / gamma flip = unpredictable direction. Wait for a clear signal before entering.
Vanna tailwind: When IV rises in a positive vanna environment, dealers buy futures = upside support. Use this to confirm long entries when IV is expanding.
